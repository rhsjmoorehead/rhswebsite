High School Courses

Grade Level Overview

Freshmen
  • English I
    Develops reading, writing, and analytical skills through literature.
  • Algebra I
    Introduces algebraic concepts including equations and functions.
  • Government
    Explores the foundations and structure of the U.S. government.
  • Physical Science
    Covers basic physics and chemistry principles.
  • Health/PE
    Focuses on fitness, health education, and physical activity.
  • 3 Pathway/Electives
    Choose three elective or pathway courses based on interests.
Sophomore
  • English II
    Enhances literary analysis and composition skills.
  • Geometry
    Focuses on geometric principles, proofs, and theorems.
  • World History
    Examines global civilizations and historical developments.
  • Biology I
    Studies cell biology, genetics, ecology, and evolution.
  • Spanish I
    Introduces basic Spanish vocabulary and grammar.
  • Sophomore Seminar
    Prepares students for post-secondary goals and careers.
  • 2 Pathway/Elective
    Select two additional elective or pathway courses.
Junior
  • English III
    Focuses on American literature and advanced writing skills.
  • Advanced Algebra
    Continues algebraic concepts with more complex functions.
  • Economics
    Teaches economic principles and personal finance basics.
  • Chemistry
    Explores chemical reactions, bonding, and laboratory techniques.
  • Spanish II
    Expands vocabulary and communication in Spanish.
  • 3 Pathway/Electives
    Students choose three courses from their pathway or electives.
Senior
  • English IV
    Emphasizes critical reading and advanced essay writing.
  • College Algebra (DE)
    Dual enrollment course for advanced algebra concepts.
  • U.S. History I & II (DE)
    Dual enrollment covering key U.S. historical events.
  • Biology II
    Advanced biology including anatomy, physiology, and biochemistry.
  • 4 Pathway/Elective
    Select four electives or pathway courses to complete graduation requirements.
